# Dormant Account Activation

---

## Metadata

- **Rule ID:** `CORR-6D`
- **Tier:** 6 — Novelty and Anomaly Detection
- **Author:** Detection Engineering
- **Description:** Detect alerts generated by accounts that have been inactive (dormant) for 90 or more days. Dormant accounts are high-value targets for attackers: they often retain elevated privileges from former roles, lack MFA enforcement, and their activity is rarely scrutinized. An account dormant for 200 days that suddenly fires a "Suspicious Login" alert is a stronger signal than the same alert from an active daily user.
- **Join Key(s):** `user.name`
- **Lookback:** 24 hours
- **Schedule:** Every 1 hour
- **Priority:** P2
- **Integration:** [alerts](https://www.elastic.co/guide/en/security/current/alerts-ui-manage.html)
- **Language:** `[ES|QL]`

## Query

```esql
FROM .internal.alerts-security.alerts-default
| WHERE @timestamp > NOW() - 24 HOURS
    AND kibana.alert.workflow_status == "open"
    AND user.name IS NOT NULL
| EVAL
    severity_weight = CASE(
        signal.rule.severity == "critical", 25,
        signal.rule.severity == "high", 15,
        signal.rule.severity == "medium", 8,
        signal.rule.severity == "low", 3, 1
    ),
    bbr_factor = CASE(kibana.alert.rule.building_block_type == "default", 0.3, 1.0),
    alert_risk = ROUND(severity_weight * bbr_factor),
    domain_category = CASE(
        event.dataset LIKE "endpoint*" OR event.dataset LIKE "sentinelone*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "windows*" OR event.dataset LIKE "sysmon*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "microsoft-defender*" OR event.dataset LIKE "crowdstrike*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "carbon_black*", "endpoint",
        event.dataset LIKE "okta*" OR event.dataset LIKE "azure_ad*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "entra*" OR event.dataset LIKE "onelogin*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "ping*" OR event.dataset LIKE "auth0*", "identity",
        event.dataset LIKE "aws*" OR event.dataset LIKE "gcp*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "azure*" OR event.dataset LIKE "cloud*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "o365*", "cloud",
        event.dataset LIKE "panw*" OR event.dataset LIKE "fortinet*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "firewall*" OR event.dataset LIKE "paloalto*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "checkpoint*", "network_fw",
        event.dataset LIKE "extrahop*" OR event.dataset LIKE "zeek*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "suricata*" OR event.dataset LIKE "ndr*", "network_ndr",
        event.dataset LIKE "proxy*" OR event.dataset LIKE "zscaler*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "bluecoat*" OR event.dataset LIKE "squid*", "proxy",
        event.dataset LIKE "dns*", "dns",
        event.dataset LIKE "email*" OR event.dataset LIKE "proofpoint*"
            OR event.dataset LIKE "mimecast*", "email",
        COALESCE(labels.technology, event.module, "unknown")
    )
| LOOKUP JOIN lookup-dormant-accounts ON user.name
| WHERE last_activity_date IS NOT NULL
    AND DATE_DIFF("day", last_activity_date, NOW()) >= 90
| STATS
    Esql.alert_count = COUNT(*),
    Esql.risk_score = SUM(alert_risk),
    Esql.max_severity = MAX(severity_weight),
    Esql.days_dormant = MAX(DATE_DIFF("day", last_activity_date, NOW())),
    Esql.first_seen = MIN(@timestamp),
    Esql.last_seen = MAX(@timestamp),
    Esql.unique_rules = COUNT_DISTINCT(kibana.alert.rule.name),
    Esql.rule_names = VALUES(kibana.alert.rule.name),
    Esql.domain_count = COUNT_DISTINCT(domain_category),
    Esql.domain_values = VALUES(domain_category),
    Esql.tactic_values = VALUES(kibana.alert.rule.threat.tactic.name),
    Esql.host_values = VALUES(host.name),
    Esql.source_ips = VALUES(source.ip)
  BY user.name
| EVAL
    Esql.correlation_severity = CASE(
        Esql.days_dormant >= 365, "critical",
        Esql.days_dormant >= 180, "high",
        Esql.days_dormant >= 90, "medium",
        "low"
    ),
    Esql.description = CONCAT(
        "DORMANT ACCOUNT ", user.name,
        " reactivated after ", TO_STRING(Esql.days_dormant), " days of inactivity",
        " | ", TO_STRING(Esql.alert_count), " alerts from ",
        TO_STRING(Esql.unique_rules), " rules",
        " | ", TO_STRING(Esql.domain_count), " domains",
        " | Risk: ", TO_STRING(Esql.risk_score)
    )
| SORT Esql.days_dormant DESC, Esql.risk_score DESC
| LIMIT 50
```

## Strategy

Each alert's `user.name` is checked against `lookup-dormant-accounts` via `LOOKUP JOIN` to retrieve `last_active_date` and `account_status`. Accounts with `last_active_date` older than 90 days are flagged. The rule computes `days_dormant` and uses it as a severity multiplier -- the longer an account has been dormant, the more suspicious its sudden activation. Aggregation per user captures the full scope of dormant account activity within the lookback window.

## Severity Logic

| Condition | Severity |
|-----------|----------|
| Account dormant for 365+ days (1 year) | Critical |
| Account dormant for 180+ days (6 months) | High |
| Account dormant for 90+ days (3 months) | Medium |

## Notes

- **Blind Spots:**
  - **Accounts not in lookup**: Users missing from `lookup-dormant-accounts` are invisible to this rule. The lookup must be comprehensive and regularly refreshed.
  - **Partial dormancy data**: If `last_activity_date` tracks only authentication events, accounts that were active in non-auth contexts (file shares, automated jobs) may be incorrectly classified as dormant.
  - **Account reuse**: If a dormant account is legitimately reactivated and the lookup is updated immediately, the window for detection is narrow.

- **False Positives:**
  - **Seasonal contractors returning**: Contractors who work seasonally (e.g., tax season, audit periods) have legitimate dormancy gaps. Mitigation: tag seasonal accounts in `lookup-dormant-accounts` with expected return windows.
  - **Reactivated service accounts for maintenance**: Service accounts spun up for quarterly maintenance. Mitigation: register expected maintenance windows.
  - **Former employee accounts used for knowledge transfer**: HR-approved temporary reactivation. Mitigation: integrate with HR ticketing for approved reactivations.

- **Tuning:**
  1. `days_dormant` severity thresholds (90/180/365) -- adjust based on your account lifecycle policies
  2. Exclude accounts with `account_status == "disabled"` if disabled accounts cannot authenticate
  3. Add a minimum `alert_risk` threshold for high-noise environments
  4. Update `lookup-dormant-accounts` daily from IAM/AD last-logon attributes
  5. Consider adding domain_count as a severity multiplier (dormant + multi-domain = escalate)

## Data Requirements

- **Index**: `.internal.alerts-security.alerts-default`
- **Lookup Index**: `lookup-dormant-accounts` (fields: `user.name`, `last_activity_date`, `account_status`)
- **Required fields**: `user.name`, `signal.rule.severity`, `kibana.alert.rule.building_block_type`, `kibana.alert.workflow_status`, `@timestamp`, `event.dataset`, `kibana.alert.rule.name`, `kibana.alert.rule.threat.tactic.name`, `host.name`, `source.ip`
- **Minimum volume**: Dormant accounts lookup populated from IAM with accurate last-activity dates

## Dependencies

- **Required**: `lookup-dormant-accounts` -- must be populated and maintained from IAM/AD systems
- **Optional**: `lookup-critical-assets` for escalation if the dormant account has elevated privileges

## Validation

1. Identify or create a test account that has not been active for 120+ days in `lookup-dormant-accounts`
2. Use that account to trigger an alert (e.g., failed authentication, process execution)
3. Verify CORR-6D surfaces the account with `Esql.days_dormant >= 120` and severity "medium" (90+ threshold)
4. Confirm that an account active yesterday does NOT appear in CORR-6D results

## Elastic Comparison

Elastic does not ship a dormant account activation correlation rule. Active Directory health rules exist for "inactive accounts" but operate on raw AD data, not alert correlation. CORR-6D is unique in combining account dormancy context with alert severity and multi-domain spread.
